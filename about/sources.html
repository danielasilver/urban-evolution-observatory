Urban Evolution Observatory

Sources

The Observatory draws on the Toronto Urban Evolution Model papers and on public, research-ready datasets that can be connected to urban propositions. This page gives readers a public map of the main source families without exposing private research files.

Toronto Urban Evolution Model

Towards a Model of Urban Evolution: Part I: Context
Introduces the need for an evolutionary model of urban life and develops the idea of the formeme.
Towards a Model of Urban Evolution: Part II: Formal Model
Defines the formal model of urban signatures, physical form, groups, activities, and distance.
Towards a Model of Urban Evolution: Part III: Rules of Evolution
Develops the model rules of variation, selection, retention, recombination, longevity, fidelity, fecundity, and trajectory.
Towards a Model of Urban Evolution Part IV: Evolutionary Formetic Distance
Applies TUEM to Yelp data to compare places and trace change over time.

Evidence Sources

Current public reports use tract-level and metropolitan comparisons derived from the research repository. Public pages report the claim, measure logic, tables, and uncertainty. Private source paths, working directories, and run logs are not part of the public observatory.

When a new dataset becomes public-facing, the source note should explain what it observes directly, what it approximates, its unit and time coverage, and which TUEM concepts it can test.

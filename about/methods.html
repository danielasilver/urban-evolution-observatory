Urban Evolution Observatory

Methods

The Observatory studies urban evolution by translating claims about cities into observable comparisons. A report begins with a proposition, identifies the part of the city that should make the claim visible, and then states what the available data can directly measure and what they can only approximate.

The first preview test uses tract-level sequence windows. It compares tracts within the same metropolitan area, so the evidence is not mainly a comparison between different metros. The question is whether tracts with more earlier movement in activity sectors and group context later show more recombination-oriented street-network change, after accounting for inherited street structure.

In this test, physical form is measured through road-network change. Groups are measured through broad social-context movement. Activities are measured through broad establishment-sector movement. These are useful first measures, but they are not complete measures of urban recombination. A later activity-recombination outcome still needs to be built from finer-grained establishment and use combinations.

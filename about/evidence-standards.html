Evidence Standards

Every public report should make the claim understandable before judging it. The report should explain what a reader should picture, what the data observe, how the test works, and what would count as support or challenge.

A finding is treated as strong only when the result is visible, substantively meaningful, and consistent with the measurement strategy. A finding is treated as mixed when the direction, magnitude, or measurement fit is not enough to carry the claim. A contrary result is not hidden in a caveat; it is used to revise the proposition.

The first preview result is mixed in this sense. Earlier group and activity movement do not clearly predict later street-network recombination in the tested windows. Inherited street structure does. The appropriate theory response is not to discard the broader idea of recombination, but to split the claim into a physical branch and an activity branch.

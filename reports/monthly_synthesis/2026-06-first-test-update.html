What The First Test Changed

The first preview result changed the shape of the claim. The starting idea was that earlier movement in physical form, groups, and activities would make later urban recombination more likely. The first test looked at one branch of that idea: later recombination-oriented street-network change.

That branch did not behave as the broad claim expected. Earlier activity-sector movement and earlier group-context movement were weak in the two high-coverage windows. Inherited street structure was much stronger.

The theory response is to split the proposition. One branch should study physical recombination and treat inherited form as an active constraint. The other branch should study activity recombination directly, using finer-grained evidence on establishments, routines, and uses.

This is the point of a self-revising research program: a result answers one question and changes which question should be asked next.
